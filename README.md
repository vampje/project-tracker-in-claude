# Project Tracker System for Product Managers

**Created by:** Doni Gross, PM for TM1 Reports at Ticketmaster
**Shared with:** Fellow PMs looking for project organization systems

---

## What This Is

A lightweight markdown-based project tracking system designed for Product Managers who need to:
- Track 7+ concurrent projects with different priorities and timelines
- Provide quick status updates to leadership
- Maintain a running log of daily work
- Manage bi-weekly JIRA epic updates for executive visibility

This system works alongside (not replacing) JIRA, Confluence, and other tools. Think of it as your **personal command center** that connects everything together.

---

## The System (4 Files)

### 1. **Dashboard** (`dashboard.md`)
**Purpose:** Single source of truth for all active projects
**Update Frequency:** As needed (typically 1-2x per week)
**Best For:** Understanding project status at a glance, tracking critical deadlines

**What it includes:**
- Priority-ordered project list (1-7 for Q2 critical path)
- Status indicators (🟢 On Track, 🔵 In Progress, 🟡 At Risk, 🔴 Blocked)
- Timeline and owner for each project
- Links to product briefs, decision docs, JIRA epics
- Next actions and blockers
- Recent updates and communication notes

**Why it's useful:**
- Quickly answer "what's the status of Project X?"
- See dependencies between projects
- Track what needs attention this week
- Prepare for leadership meetings

---

### 2. **Daily Log** (`daily-log.md`)
**Purpose:** Running chronological record of what you worked on
**Update Frequency:** Daily (end of day or next morning)
**Best For:** Remembering what you did, tracking follow-ups, weekly summaries

**What it includes:**
- Date-stamped entries (newest at top)
- Work completed that day
- Meetings attended
- Next actions identified
- Blockers encountered
- Status changes

**Why it's useful:**
- Answer "what did I do on March 15?"
- Track follow-up items over time
- Generate weekly summaries quickly
- Document decisions and conversations

---

### 3. **Weekly Updates for Leadership** (`weekly-updates.md`)
**Purpose:** Table format that copies directly into leadership docs
**Update Frequency:** As you complete work (copy to leader's doc weekly)
**Best For:** Status reporting, demonstrating progress

**What it includes:**
- Week, Who, Goal, Description, Priority, Status, Asset/Doc
- Copy-paste ready format for Google Docs/Sheets
- Archive section for historical reference

**Why it's useful:**
- 2-minute update process (copy rows to leader's doc)
- Clear format executives can scan quickly
- Historical record of accomplishments
- Easy to show progress over time

---

### 4. **Bi-Weekly JIRA Epic Updates** (`bi-weekly-jira-updates-workflow.md`)
**Purpose:** Systematic workflow for updating JIRA epics for executive visibility
**Update Frequency:** Every 2 weeks
**Best For:** Keeping leadership informed on progress without meetings

**What it includes:**
- Step-by-step workflow
- RAG status updates (On Track / At Risk / Off Track)
- Executive-friendly update format
- Milestone aggregation rules

**Why it's useful:**
- Turns 2-hour manual update into 30-minute conversation (with AI assistant)
- Consistent format across all epics
- Clear visibility into project health
- Milestone rollups for program-level status

## How to Adapt This System

### Getting Started
1. **Copy the template files** to your own directory
2. **Customize dashboard.md** with your Q2/Q3 projects
3. **Start daily logging** to build the habit
4. **Set up weekly updates** format with your manager

### Customization Tips
- **Adjust priority levels** to match your organization (1-10 vs 1-7)
- **Change status indicators** to match your team's language
- **Add/remove sections** based on what you track
- **Use your own labels** (H1 2026 vs Q2, etc.)

### With AI Assistants (Claude, ChatGPT, Copilot)
This system is designed to work seamlessly with AI:
- **AI can update files** based on your conversations
- **AI can query files** to answer "what's the status of X?"
- **AI can generate updates** by reading your daily log
- **AI can format JIRA tickets** from your notes

My workflow: I tell Claude what I worked on, and it updates my daily log and dashboard automatically.

## Tips from Experience

**What Works:**
- Update daily log at end of day while fresh in your mind
- Use emojis for visual scanning (🟢🔵🟡🔴 for status, 🎯📋🗓️ for task types)
- Link everything (JIRA tickets, Confluence pages, Google Docs)
- Archive completed work to keep files manageable
- Use consistent date format (YYYY-MM-DD) for easy sorting

**What Doesn't Work:**
- Trying to track EVERYTHING (focus on what matters for status updates)
- Letting files get stale (update or it's useless)
- Complex formatting (keep it simple markdown)
- Duplicating what's already in JIRA (link instead)

**Time Investment:**
- Daily log: 5 minutes per day
- Dashboard updates: 10-15 minutes twice per week
- Weekly updates: 2 minutes to copy rows
- Bi-weekly JIRA updates: 30 minutes every 2 weeks

**Total:** ~1 hour per week for complete project visibility
